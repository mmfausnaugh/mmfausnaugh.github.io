A key aspect of my research is studying and improving observational methods, particularly extraction and uses of high precision spectrophotometry.

TESS needs to be able to detect transiting planet signatures of order 100 ppm or smaller, and I have spent the last three years leading the DAWG to understand the dominant systematic errors in TESS and tune the mission's data reduction pipeline to ge the best quality data possible.

My personal research focuses on using TESS for faint, extragalactic sources. With difference imaging techniques, we've been able to get 19.5 three sigma detections in the 30 minute TESS FFIs, and we have a good account of the dominante systematic errors in the data.

My distertation was focused on high SNR spectrophotometry of AGN, in order to measure black hole masses. I wrote a python module and paper updating the standard methods for internight calibration. We did novel things like measure the temperature profile of the famous Seyfert Galaxy NGC 5548.
